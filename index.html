  • Major: Media Arts

A Game Memory

My earliest video game memory is playing a browser based platformer called Tamale Loco. Taco Loco was a mouse who in efforts to win over the father of his crush, was collecting bizarre ingredients such as strawberries and sardines to make burritos. I spent countless hours playing this on dial-up internet in my parents home office. For as long as I can remember, I have loved games of all kinds and have been curious about what went in to making them.

How I See the World

I see the world as a web of interconnected stories. Games reflect that. They let us experiment with choices, consequences, and identities in a safe space. I think games can help us understand complex problems by letting us play with them and allow us to exercise empathy in an immersive way.
